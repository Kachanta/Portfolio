About me

My journey began early, fueled by a self-taught exploration of computers at just 12 years old. This passion grew into something tangible at 19, when I founded "Excel Internet Cafe" in Kujama. It wasn't just a business; it was a bridge over the digital divide, slashing internet costs and training over 30 young people in crucial computer skills.


Then, the pandemic brought an unexpected turn. While teaching math at Adventist College Kujama, my hunger for intellectual challenge led me to discover data science. The magic of extracting insights from hidden patterns, of using data to tell stories and solve problems, captivated me instantly.


With my background in software development and a strong foundation in mathematics, I dived headfirst into online courses. Armed with practical tools like Python, Tableau, and MySQL, I honed my skills in data collection, analysis, and visualization. I can confidently navigate advanced operations like feature engineering and machine learning modeling.


But my journey is far from over. I'm currently pursuing a Master's in Statistics and Data Analysis at the Nigerian Defense Academy, pushing my boundaries to make a bigger impact.


The potential of data to shape a better world drives me. From optimizing operations to improving healthcare and understanding social trends, I believe data can lead us towards a more informed and equitable future.


So, if you're looking for a data scientist who thrives on challenges, brings a unique blend of academic rigor and real-world experience, and possesses a deep understanding of the human impact of technology, then let's connect! I'm eager to collaborate, learn, and contribute to projects that make a difference.
